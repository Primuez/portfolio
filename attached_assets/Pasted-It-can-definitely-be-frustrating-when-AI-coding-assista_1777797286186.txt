### The "Technical Blueprint" Prompt for Replit

**Copy and paste this into Replit:**

> "I want to build a full-screen, interactive vertical scrolling website with a deep 3D parallax effect, similar to high-end Slider Revolution templates. Please use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, leveraging **GSAP (GreenSock)** and the **ScrollTrigger** plugin for smooth animations. 
>
> **Here are the specific requirements:**
> 
> **1. Layout & Structure:**
> * Create 4 full-screen sections (`100vh` and `100vw`).
> * Each section must have multiple absolute-positioned layers to create depth: a background layer (e.g., sky), a mid-ground layer (e.g., clouds, planets), a foreground subject (e.g., a car or astronaut), and a UI/Text layer.
> 
> **2. The Parallax Scroll Animation:**
> * Tie the scroll event to the animation so the user controls the timeline by scrolling.
> * As the user scrolls to the next section, the layers must move on the Y-axis at **different speeds**. 
> * The background should move the slowest, the mid-ground faster, and the foreground subject the fastest. This creates the 'magical' 3D depth effect.
> 
> **3. Text & Typography:**
> * Make the typography large, modern, and bold. 
> * When a new slide enters the viewport, the text should stagger in, fading from `opacity: 0` and sliding up from a lower Y position with smooth easing.
> 
> **4. Responsiveness:**
> * Ensure the animations and layout are completely fluid and responsive for both mobile and desktop. 
> * Touch-swiping on mobile should trigger the same smooth parallax transitions as the mouse wheel does on a desktop.
> 
> Please write the code for this, structure the layers clearly so I can swap in my own transparent PNG images later, and ensure GSAP is properly imported."

### Why this prompt works better:
* **Names the specific tools:** Telling it to use **GSAP and ScrollTrigger** gives the AI the exact industry-standard library needed for this kind of "magical" scroll binding. Native CSS is often too clunky for this specific effect.
* **Explains the physics:** Instead of saying "magically appear," it tells the AI *how* the magic works (moving layers at different speeds on the Y-axis).
* **Prepares for your assets:** It asks the AI to set up the code so you can easily drop in your own transparent images (like the car, planets, or clouds from your video) later.

**Pro-Tip for your images:** To get this effect to look as good as the video, you will need your images separated into layers. For example, your subject (like the car or astronaut) needs to be a transparent PNG with no background, so the background layers can slide independently behind it!
